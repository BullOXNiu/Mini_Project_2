    How many times did teams win within a season?


    First focusing on the season 2019-2020. The average number of wins per team throughout a season was 14.4 with some teams exceeding that average by a lot, while others the opposite. The teams’ wins are shown in the visualization below, ranked by best performing teams to worst performing.

    The visualization shows that the best team of the 2019-2020 season was Liverpool with a total of 32 wins and the worst team of the season was Norwich with a total of 5 wins.

    We also used a parameter that allows us to choose between the three seasons that we are interested in, 2017-2018, 2018-2019, and 2019-2020. So, by changing the season we can see the ranking of the teams for that season. The highlight for the Team Name is also useful as it enables us to emphasize on the performance of a specific team of interest.

    How many goals are scored on average every game?



    Another interesting point on our data is the number of goals scored in each game. Each team plays 38 matches so there are a total of 380 games every season, so the visualization of the average goals per game became very long. However, the average goals scored is clearly indicated on the graph, and is equal to 2.72 average goals per game in the season 2019-2020. To show the average goals we used the game ID’s (“Game”) which ranges from 1 to 380 to separate one game from another. As there can be multiple games on the same date, so the date didn’t work as a good identifier. Again, we can interact with the visualization by changing the season using the season parameter.

    Do teams win most games at home or away?

    We were also interested to compare the performance of a team when it is playing in their home stadium or away. We predicted that the performance would be significantly better at home than away. To check this we created two visualizations, one showing the teams’ wins when playing at home, and the other the teams’ wins when playing away.



    These two visualizations are purposely not sorted in an increasing or decreasing order of the wins, but it is sorted alphabetically. We chose this sort as it allows us to compare the teams more easily when looking at the two bar graphs stacked on top of each other. The visualization clearly shows that teams are performing worse in away stadiums than in home stadiums. The average number of wins per team playing at home is 8.6 while the average number of wins per team when playing away is 5.8. Also, looking at individual teams it is clear that their performance increases when playing at home. Liverpool, the best team of the season 2019-2020, won 18 games at home and 14 games away. Looking at the other end, Norwich, worst team of the season, won 4 games at home, and only 1 away.

    Topic Two: Referee

    Does the referee favor the home team more than the away team?


    Are referees more likely to give out yellow or red cards to the home or away team?



    Since it is likely that other factors such as home crowd had an impact on the outcome of EPL matches, we then analyzed the number of yellow or red cards shown by individual referees.

    In particular, we computed a custom metric by first subtracting the total numbers of cards shown divided by the total number of fouls given. Next, we take that number and divide it further based on the home and away teams. Finally, we measure the difference between home and away teams in terms of the likelihood of any given foul resulting in a yellow or red card.

    We then decided to use this parameter and apply it to the previous graph, in the form of a color overlay.

    Finally, we computed the likelihood of referees giving out red cards after a foul and applied it as a color overlay to this current graph. Unlike yellow cards, red cards are usually far more game-changing in soccer, we added this layer of information to see whether some referees are more probable of producing a red card from fouls than others.

    Topic Four: Covid Break

    Due to the coronavirus outbreak, the 2019-20 EPL season got suspended from March 13 to Jun 17. As it is extremely rare to have such an extended break during mid-season, we were curious to see if this unexpected event has caused a drop in form to the teams competing. Based on results from the previous topic, we decided to analyze the difference between the number of goals scored in the first half and those in the second half. Additionally, we wanted to study the change in total shots and total shots on target, which serves as another metric on team performance. Perhaps a lack of competitions has led to either fewer shots on target or, equally likely, more goals conceded since players were allowed extra rest during a typically intense EPL season.


    We first created a stacked bar chart to show the number of shots on target on top of total shots taken. Right beneath it is our goal difference chart, which showed the number of goals scored in each half of a match. We’ve also shaded the area after the coronavirus break has ended, and we can choose to focus on either part of the season using the coronavirus break as a natural separator.

    Mean values for total shots and total shots on target both went down after the break; while teams scored/conceded more goals in the first half than in the second half due to the break. From the trend lines, we also observed that after the initial “shock”, teams were able to bounce back fairly quickly. In terms of goal difference in the two halves, it remains unclear whether the slight increase in goals scored in the first half was due to the coronavirus break or other unexplained variables. Notably, teams tend to play youth players more often toward the end of a season, when the league table began to stabilize.
